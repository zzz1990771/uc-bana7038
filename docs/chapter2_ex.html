Exercise 1

For the Chicago data set, do the following analysis.

  • Read data into R, use y as response and x as covariate to run a simple linear regression.

  • Obtain parameter estimates of slope and intercept.

  • Obtain the fitted values, compare the fitted values with the actual values by plotting one against the other.

  • Obtain the residuals, and visualize its distribution.

  • What are the standard error of the slops and intercept. Are they too large?

  • Perform hypothesis testing for the slope, i.e., \(H_0: \beta_1=0\). what is your conclusion?

  • Do the same thing for the intercept, i.e., \(H_0: \beta_0=0\)

  • Obtain the interval estimation of slope and intercept. Can you spot the connection between interval estimation and hypothesis testing in the previous questions? If you cannot, continue with the rest of the data set.

  • What does attach(...) function do? Why do we need it?

  • What does abline(h=...) do?

d=read.csv("data_chicago.csv")
names(d)
